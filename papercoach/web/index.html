1 Introduction

Sequence transduction problems are commonly formulated with an encoder-decoder approach [Sutskever et al., 2014, Cho et al., 2014]. The best performing models also connect the encoder and decoder through an attention mechanism.

However, recurrent models suffer from an inherent sequential computation limitation that precludes parallel training within sequence positions, which becomes critical at longer sequence lengths. 第 1.2 节

Attention, as an alternative, has been shown to be an effective mechanism in practice for sequence modeling and transduction.

In this work, we combine these observations and propose a new simple network architecture, the Transformer, based solely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions entirely.

Experiments on two machine translation tasks show these models to be superior in quality while being more parallelizable, requiring substantially less time to train.
